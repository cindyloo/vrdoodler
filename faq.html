Photosphere

What is a photopshere? Essentially, it's your panoramic picture wrapped around a giant sphere with you in the center.
Click the "Photosphere" radio button and Load a JPG or PNG file.
Different from Google Storyspheres, you can annotate your photosphere using VRDoodler.

Drawing

Key principle: use SHIFT to draw on the same plane as a previous line. Use the mouse to target a particular line.
Hold SHIFT, press the mouse down to start drawing. Otherwise your new line will draw at the current grid coordinate.

By drawing AT the intersection of another line, you 'group' those lines together. This is useful for copy and pasting.

  • {CTRL-c} to copy
  • {CTRL-v} to paste
  • {CTRL-z} to erase previous line
  • {BACKSPACE/DELETE} to erase currently selected line

Snapping
Note how the intersection point color changes depending on object intersected.
  • a yellow dot when intersecting with the grid
  • a red dot when intersecting with another line
  • a green dot when drawing at the plane specified by the previous intersection
  • use SHIFT to draw on the same plane as a previous line: use the mouse to target(hover over) a particular line.
    Hold SHIFT, press the mouse down to start drawing. Otherwise your new line will draw at the current grid coordinate.
  • You can turn snapping off in advanced mode.

use the CTRL(or cmd), SHIFT and ALT keys to draw along specific axes, or to draw straight lines.

  • {CTRL-g} to draw flat along horizon
  • {CTRL-SHIFT-g} to draw a straight vertical line
  • {CTRL-ALT-g} to draw a straight horizontal line
  • {ALT-g} to draw flat along world z axis

Rotation

Toggle drawing off. Use the mouse to rotate the camera view. IF you have targeted a point with Snap Mode on, you will be able to rotate
AROUND that targeted intersection instead of around the origin
